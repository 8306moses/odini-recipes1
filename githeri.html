githeri recipe

Githeri is a traditional Kenyan dish made from a mixture of boiled maize and beans. It is a nutritious and hearty meal that is easy to prepare. Here’s a simple recipe to make githeri:

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups dried maize (corn)
  • 2 cups dried beans (red kidney beans or black beans)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon salt (to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Water (for boiling)

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the dried maize and beans thoroughly. Soak them in water overnight to soften.
  2. Drain the soaked maize and beans. In a large pot, add the maize and beans and cover them with water. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for about 1.5 to 2 hours, or until both the maize and beans are tender. You may need to add more water during cooking to keep them submerged.
  3. In a separate pan, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, and sauté until they are soft and fragrant.
  4. Add the chopped tomatoes and green bell pepper to the pan. Cook for about 5-7 minutes, until the vegetables are tender.
  5. Add the cooked maize and beans to the pan with the sautéed vegetables. Stir well to combine.
  6. Season the mixture with salt, black pepper, and curry powder (if using). Cook for an additional 10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  7. Serve the githeri hot, either on its own or with a side of ugali or chapati.
