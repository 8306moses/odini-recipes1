ugalis Recipes

Description

Ugali is a popular East African staple food made from maize flour and water. It is cooked until thick and firm, forming a smooth, dough-like consistency. Ugali is often served with vegetables, meat, or stew and is known for its simplicity, affordability, and ability to keep you full for long. It is a traditional dish enjoyed in many homes across Kenya and neighboring countries.

Ingredients

  • maize flour
  • water
  • blueband
  • heat source

steps

  1. Boil water in a pot.
  2. add blueband to the boiling water
  3. Gradually add maize flour to the boiling water while stirring continuously to avoid lumps.
  4. Continue stirring until the mixture thickens and pulls away from the sides of the pot.
  5. Reduce heat and cover the pot. Let it cook for a few more minutes.
  6. Remove from heat and let it sit for a couple of minutes.
  7. Scoop the ugali onto a plate and shape it into a mound using a wooden spoon or your hands.
  8. Add a knob of blueband on top for extra flavor (optional).
  9. Serve hot with your favorite side dishes like vegetables, meat, or stew.
